# .impeccable.md — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Primarily technical recruiters, engineering hiring managers, and potential collaborators (other engineers, researchers). They arrive with intent — they've been linked here or are researching Charlie. Context is professional but the medium invites exploration. The job to be done: quickly assess competence, get a feel for the person, decide whether to reach out.

### Brand Personality
**Precise · Quiet · Sharp**

Dark, technical, and understated without being cold. Confident and direct like a well-written README — no filler, no selling. The experience itself demonstrates craft; the copy stays out of the way. Think: a senior engineer's personal toolkit, not a marketing site.

### Emotional Goals
The visitor should feel all of: impressed by the craft of the experience, intrigued by the person behind it, confident in the competence on display, and lightly delighted by the novelty. None of these feelings should require reading anything — the 3D solar system communicates them before a single panel opens.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only. Near-black background (`#050508`), warm off-white text (`#e8e4dc`), muted secondary text (`#6b6760`), warm parchment-gold accent (`#c4b99a`).
- **Visual tone**: Minimalist technical illustration — thin outlines, sparse geometry, careful negative space. Inspired by architectural drawings and scientific diagrams, not gaming UIs.
- **Typography**: DM Sans (weight 300/400) for readable body copy; Space Mono for structural labels, headings, and anything that should feel like a terminal or code comment.
- **Motion**: Purposeful and smooth — GSAP tweens, subtle easing, nothing gratuitous. Reduced-motion users get a fully functional no-animation experience.

**Anti-references** (explicitly avoid):
- Neon/cyberpunk aesthetic — purple/pink glows, aggressive "hacker" energy
- Generic dev portfolio template — Bootstrap hero, card grid, feels like a theme
- Overly corporate SaaS landing page — polished but soulless

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint earns attention.** Every element on screen competes for focus. Add nothing that doesn't earn its place. When in doubt, remove.

2. **The scene is the statement.** The 3D solar system IS the design. Panels and UI chrome support it — they don't compete with it. Keep panels clean, minimal, and fast to read.

3. **Warm, not cold.** Dark doesn't mean sterile. The gold accent, off-white text, and subtle warm tints in the nebula/panel backgrounds prevent the interface from feeling clinical or intimidating.

4. **Precision over polish.** Pixel-perfect alignment, consistent spacing, and typographic discipline matter more than decorative effects. Space Mono carries the "technical" signal; let it.

5. **Works everywhere, invisibly.** Mobile, tablet, desktop, reduced motion — the experience degrades gracefully without calling attention to it. Cross-device quality is a baseline, not a feature.
